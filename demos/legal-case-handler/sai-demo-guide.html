Legal Case Handler — Demo Guide

For Sai · Keep this open while recording on Claude.ai

Before you start

This demo shows Claude.ai Projects handling a real legal threat. You'll film yourself in Claude.ai while following these steps. The goal: audience sees AI go from messy legal threat → clear verdict in 2 minutes.

1 Set up the Claude Project

Open claude.ai → click "Projects" in sidebar → "New Project"

Fill in these details:

Name: Legal Case Handler

Description: Reviews incoming legal threats and recommends action

2 Add the system instruction

In the Project settings, paste this into the system instruction field:

COPY THIS
You are a legal risk analyst specialising in Australian business law. When given a legal document or threat, you must: 1. Identify the jurisdiction and applicable law 2. Assess the legal merit on a scale of 1-10 (1 = no basis, 10 = strong claim) 3. Estimate potential financial exposure 4. Recommend one of three actions: IGNORE / RESPOND / FIRE A LAWYER 5. Draft a brief response if recommending RESPOND Be direct and concise. Do not hedge excessively.
3 Upload the legal document

In the chat, paste this cease & desist letter:

COPY THIS
LEGAL NOTICE - Recording Consent Violation To: [Business Name] From: Hargreaves & Associates Legal Date: 12 May 2026 We act on behalf of [Client Name] regarding the alleged recording of telephone conversations without prior consent. Under the Surveillance Devices Act 2004, recording a conversation without the consent of all parties may constitute a criminal offence. The evidence indicates that you recorded at least five sales calls without informing participants of the recording. We demand you cease all recording activities immediately and pay compensation of $15,000 within 14 days or we will commence proceedings.
4 Ask Claude for the assessment

In the same message or a follow-up, ask:

COPY THIS
Review this legal threat and give me your assessment. What's the legal merit? What should I do?
5 Expected output

Claude should produce a clear verdict. Here's what you'll likely see:

EXAMPLE OUTPUT
ASSESSMENT Jurisdiction: Australia (Surveillance Devices Act 2004) Legal merit: 2/10 — single-party consent applies. Recording your own calls is lawful. Financial exposure: Low. No demonstrated damages. Speculative claim. RECOMMENDATION: IGNORE REASONING: Under the Surveillance Devices Act 2004, a party to a conversation may legally record it without notifying others. This threat misrepresents the law. Firm sending this letter (Hargreaves & Associates) has sent 47 similar letters; 0 resulted in litigation. DRAFT RESPONSE (optional): "We have reviewed your correspondence. The alleged recording consent violation has no legal basis under the Surveillance Devices Act 2004. We do not intend to engage further with this matter."

Note: The actual output will vary based on Claude's reasoning, but the structure and recommendation should be clear.
