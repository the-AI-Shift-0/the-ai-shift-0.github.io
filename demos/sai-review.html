Part 2

Copy & CRO Audit

Honest assessment of every user-facing page against copywriting and conversion best practices. What's working, what's soft, and specific fixes.

Hub Page

theaishift.biz

6 / 10

Current strength

What's Working

  • Clean, on-brand visual design — the purple gradient hero looks professional
  • Two email capture points (hero + bottom) — good practice
  • Episode cards give a taste of what's coming — curiosity driver
  • Loading state on form submit — small detail that builds trust
  • Mobile responsive

What Needs Work

  • No pain section — drops straight into the form with zero "is this for me?" moment
  • Headline is generic — "results, not theory" is a line every AI course uses
  • Pills are vague — "Cut costs / Grow revenue / Remove dependencies" could describe any software company
  • Zero social proof — no subscriber count, no "used by X business owners"
  • Bottom CTA "Get Notified" is weak — notified of what? Why now?
  • Tool descriptions are features, not outcomes — "Analyze sales calls" instead of "Stop losing deals to bad coaching"

Specific Copy Fixes

The headline and sub need to be more specific. Here's a before/after:

Current

"Practical AI for business owners who want results, not theory."

Stronger

"One AI tool per episode. Built for your business. Ready to use in 15 minutes."

Current (pills)

"Cut costs · Grow revenue · Remove dependencies"

Stronger (pills)

"Works in Claude or ChatGPT · No setup required · Personalized to your business"

  • Add a one-sentence pain hook above the form: "You don't need another AI explainer. You need a tool that works in your business today."
  • Once you have 100+ subscribers, add a trust line: "Join [X] business owners getting one free AI tool per episode."
  • Change bottom CTA "Get Notified" → "Get the Next Tool" and the sub-copy to: "New episode = new tool. Get yours the moment it drops."

Sales Analyzer Master Trove

demos/sales-analyzer/master-trove.html

7.5 / 10

Current strength

What's Working

  • 2-step form with progress reduces friction — the "Next: Your Sales Profile" flow is excellent
  • "What's Inside" section before the form sets up perceived value before the ask
  • "See Example Output" tab is a huge trust builder — lets people see what they'll get before submitting
  • Industry-specific output (coaching vs. SaaS vs. e-commerce) makes the result feel genuinely personal
  • The AI content itself is exceptional: SPIN + Challenger + Sandler frameworks, scoring rubric, objection library
  • Platform instructions (Claude + ChatGPT tabs) lower the barrier to actually using it

What Needs Work

  • Title "Sales Analyzer Master Trove" is a product name, not a benefit — a cold visitor doesn't know what a Master Trove is
  • The subtitle is just one thin sentence — no pain hook, no "who this is for"
  • No trust signal before the form — what's this from? Who made it?
  • "See Example Output" tab is buried — most users won't find it before they decide to submit
  • Button copy "Generate My Sales Report" is okay but weak — "Build My Coaching System" is stronger

Specific Fixes

Current headline

"Sales Analyzer Master Trove"

Stronger headline

"Your Custom AI Sales Coach — Built in 60 Seconds"

Current subtitle

"Tell us about your business. We'll generate a custom system prompt trained on SPIN Selling, Challenger Sale, and 10,000 sales calls."

Stronger subtitle

"Most sales managers can't review 1 in 10 calls. This AI reviews all of them — calibrated to your industry, your deal size, your reps."

  • Add a small eyebrow above the title: "Episode 01 — Free Resource" so visitors know where they are
  • Move "See Example Output" link to above the form with a teaser: "See what you're getting before you fill anything out →"
  • Change submit button: "Generate My Sales Report" → "Build My Coaching System →"

Part 3

Standard Landing Page Template

The same structure for every future episode. Not reinventing the wheel each time — just swapping in the episode-specific content. Follow this order exactly.

Each block below is a section on the page. The shade shows priority: darkest = must-have, lightest = optional. These are in order from top to bottom of the page.

1
Must-Have

HERO — Pain hook + value prop

Eyebrow: "Episode 0X — Free Resource" | Headline: benefit-focused, not tool-name | Sub: 1-2 sentences naming the problem and the outcome | CTA button: "Get My Free [Tool Name] →"

[Pain question] + [what they'll get] + [time to value]

Example: "Stop coaching calls manually. Get a custom AI sales coach calibrated to your team, your industry, and your top objection — ready in 60 seconds."

2
Must-Have

WHAT YOU GET — 3-layer breakdown

The existing "What's Inside" box. Keep it. But lead with outcomes, not features.

Layer 1 (immediate): the tool/prompt they get to use today

Layer 2 (personalized): what makes it specific to their business

Layer 3 (ongoing): what they can do with it over time

3
Strong

SEE EXAMPLE OUTPUT — Preview before the ask

Link to the example output page BEFORE the form. "See what you'll get before you fill anything out →" — reduces friction by showing the value upfront. Currently this exists as a tab, but it should be more visible.

4
Must-Have

FORM — 2-step with strong CTA

Step 1: Name + Email only. Step 2: business context. Submit button must follow the formula:

[Action] + [What They Get] + [→]

Examples: "Build My Coaching System →" / "Generate My Scripts →" / "Get My Cost Estimate →"

Avoid: "Submit", "Generate", "Get Started", "Continue".

5
Good to Have

HOW IT WORKS — 3 steps

Simple numbered steps: (1) Fill in your business profile — takes 90 seconds. (2) We generate your custom system. (3) Load it into Claude or ChatGPT and use it today. Reduces "will this be hard?" anxiety.

6
Good to Have

WHO IT'S FOR — 3 bullets

Qualify the visitor. "This is for you if..." + 3 specific situations. Also include "This is NOT for you if..." — exclusivity increases trust. Example: "Not for you if you're still figuring out your sales process. This is for businesses already doing sales calls who want to improve conversion rates."

7
Optional — Add When Available

SOCIAL PROOF

Once you have testimonials or subscriber numbers, add a strip here: "Joined by [X] business owners" or a 1-line quote from a viewer. Even a sub count once you hit 100+ is worth showing.

8
Optional

HOW TO USE — Platform instructions

The existing Claude/ChatGPT tabs — keep these. Move to below the form (they're post-conversion content, not pre-conversion persuasion).

RULE: Copy formula for every episode

Every headline follows: "[Pain avoided] without [friction they expect]" or "[Specific outcome] in [time]". Every CTA follows: "[Action verb] + [what they get]". Every "what you get" section leads with outcomes, not feature names.
