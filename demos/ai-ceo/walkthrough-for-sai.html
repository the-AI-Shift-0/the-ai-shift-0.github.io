AI Co-CEO — Walkthrough for Sai

Apurv's talking points · Use this during your call with Sai

1. The User Story

WHO: CEO of a $1–5M revenue SMB (coaching, agency, service business)

WHEN: Monday morning, 9am, before the first team call of the week

WHAT THEY SEE: One screen summarising everything that happened last week + what needs attention

What they do next (3 decisions in under 5 minutes):

  • Decision 1 - Pipeline: "Pipeline is $21K below target — do I need to get personally involved?" → click pipeline panel → see which deals are stalled → yes, follow up on 2 deals today
  • Decision 2 - Operational drag: "23 hours of my week went to operational drag — that's 30% more than my threshold. What's eating me?" → click operational drag → see the 3 recurring issues → delegate or fix
  • Decision 3 - Revenue at risk: "$20K in stalled deals needs a nudge — who do I follow up with?" → action right there

2. Why Sai Might Feel Something Is Off

The credibility gap: The demo shows Calendar/Slack/Email connected — but where does "23 hours on operational drag" actually come from? Narrate the data source. The AI is reading their calendar events, classifying them, and summing time.

The product shape confusion: The chat chips on l1 look like question prompts, but l2 is a dashboard. Make it clear: l1 is "ad-hoc questions or setup", l2 is "the automated weekly report that's always ready".

3. The Angle for SMB Owners

Every SMB CEO says the same thing: "I don't have time."

This tool gives them 5 minutes of crystal-clear clarity that replaces hours of manual review: checking Slack for blockers, reviewing the CRM, scrolling calendar, chasing team members for updates.

The frame: "Your CEO currently spends 3–4 hours every Monday just figuring out what happened last week. This gives them that in 5 minutes."

4. Suggested Talking Script

"Think about your Monday morning. What's the first thing you do? You check Slack. Then email. Then the CRM. Then you call your sales manager. This replaces all of that."
"The AI isn't writing generic insights. It's reading YOUR calendar and YOUR Slack and YOUR deals. So what comes back is actually relevant."
"The CEO focus time metric — that 40% vs 70% target — that comes from the calendar. Every meeting that isn't sales, delivery, or strategy is flagged as 'off-target'."
"Walk through the demo with me: start at l1 (the chat), then l2 (the full dashboard), then drill into l3 (operational drag) and l3b (pipeline). Each click answers a real Monday morning question."
Bottom Line: The story is: a CEO gets Monday morning clarity in 5 minutes instead of 3 hours. Every feature in the demo exists to support that story. Walk Sai through l1 → l2 → l3 → l3b in that order while narrating the Monday morning scenario. Emphasize data sources (calendar, Slack, email) to close the credibility gap.
