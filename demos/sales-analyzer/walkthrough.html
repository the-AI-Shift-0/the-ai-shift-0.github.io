Filming Guide

Sales Call Analyzer Demo

Two-part walkthrough: the built demo + what your viewers can build themselves with Claude. Follow each step on camera.

DEMO 1
The Built Analyzer (3 pages, 90 seconds total)

Team View: The Opening Move

1
Screen
Loading screen (1.4 seconds)
Say
"I never listened to a single one of these 63 calls. AI did it for me — in seconds."
Why it lands
Sets up the promise: no listening to hours of audio. The AI did the work.
2
Screen
Index.html fully loaded. Page shows: "Sales Call Analyzer" header, Team Performance title, four summary cards, seven reps listed
Say
"Here's my team. Seven reps, 63 calls, all scored. On the surface — looks fine. Everyone's in the 50s, 60s, 70s. Normal distribution."
Why it lands
The team looks healthy. Set expectations low so Kyle's red flag hits harder next.
3
Screen
Scroll/point to summary cards. Read aloud: "Team Close Rate 31% vs 38% target" · "Calls Analyzed 63" · "Avg Score 71%, down 4 points" · "1 rep flagged — repeating pattern detected"
Say
"But look at this — we're 7 points below target. Team average dropped 4 points in eight weeks. And one rep has a repeating pattern."
Why it lands
Flags the pattern before showing Kyle. Builds curiosity: which rep? What pattern?
4
Screen
Scroll down to rep list. Locate Kyle Brennan: 54%, red border, "⚠ Repeating pattern" badge, "View details →" link
Say
"There's Kyle — 54%, down 18 points in eight weeks. And AI flagged a repeating pattern. Let me see what it is."
Why it lands
Kyle is visibly worse than everyone else. The red styling and badge make it impossible to miss.
5
Action
Click "View details →" on Kyle's row
Timing
Wait 1.2s for l2.html to load

Rep Detail View: The Diagnosis

6
Screen
l2.html: Kyle's card with 54% score, trend chart (declining red line), behavior breakdown
Say
"His score dropped from 72% to 54% in eight weeks. But here's the interesting part — it's not everything. Rapport's actually OK at 72%. So what's broken?"
Why it lands
You're ruling out "Kyle is bad at sales." This is specific. Watch how the diagnosis narrows.
7
Screen
Point to score breakdown (Rapport 72% · Objection Handling 38% RED · Clarity 62% · Needs Matching 48% · Authority/Frame 50%)
Say
"Objection handling — 38%. That's the leak. Everything else is yellow or green. One skill."
Why it lands
This is the money shot: it's not a general problem. It's one specific weakness with a name.
8
Screen
Scroll down to "Recurring Weaknesses" cards. First card: "Pricing objection fumbled in 6 of last 8 calls — getting worse each week" · PATTERN badge · "View details →" link
Say
"And it's the same thing over and over — pricing objections. Same mistake, six times in eight calls. It's not random. It's a pattern."
Why it lands
Patterns are trainable. This isn't "Kyle needs to improve." It's "Kyle needs one specific coaching session."
9
Action
Click on the pricing weakness card or "View details →"
Timing
Wait 1.2s for l3.html to load

Call Detail: The Moment

10
Screen
l3.html: "Discovery call — May 8, 2026 · 34 min" · Prospect: Operations Director · Deal size: $12K · Score: 48%
Say
"Here's one call. Thirty-four minutes. I didn't listen to a second of it."
Why it lands
Reinforces: you're not doing this manually. AI extracted the exact moments from 34 minutes of audio.
11
Screen
RED banner: "⚠ Same pricing objection pattern as 5 previous calls - this is the 6th occurrence in 8 weeks"
Say
"AI flagged this as the sixth time Kyle hit this exact pattern."
Why it lands
Cross-call pattern detection. This isn't one bad call — it's systematic.
12
Screen
Key Moments timeline. Locate 17:31 moment with label: "That seems like a lot for our current situation" → rep adds third feature. Tone shifts ("Lost here")
Say
"Here's the moment — 17:31. Prospect says 'That seems like a lot for our current situation.' That's a budget concern. Kyle adds a third feature. And here — the tone shifts. Prospect disengages."
Why it lands
You're showing, not telling. The exact timestamp, the exact words, the exact wrong move.
✨
WOW Moment
The 17:31 clip is the payoff. AI extracted the exact second where the call was lost from 34 minutes of audio. Without listening.
13
Screen
Scroll to "Call Assessment" or "Coaching Note" section
Say
"And here's the thing — this call was lost because Kyle redirected to features three times when the prospect raised budget. He never addressed the price."
Why it lands
Root cause identified. Clear, actionable diagnosis.
14
Screen
Read coaching note: "This is not a general training issue. It is one specific skill gap: pricing confidence. Do not send to general sales training. One focused session on price confrontation will move the needle faster."
Say
"And here's the coaching note — this isn't Kyle needing sales training. One focused session on price confrontation. That's it."
Why it lands
This is the payoff. The demo delivers what every sales manager wants: specific, actionable coaching. Not generic advice.

Optional: Compare View

15
Screen
Go back to index.html. Click "Compare" tab (top of page)
Say
"And if you want to see what top performers do differently, the Compare view shows Sarah — 82%, up nine points — side by side with Kyle."
Why it lands
Optional but strong: shows the contrast. What's working vs what's broken.
DEMO 2
What Your Viewers Can Build (Claude or ChatGPT, 2 minutes)

Getting Started: Open Claude

1
Screen
Open Claude.ai in browser. Click "New conversation"
Say
"Now, here's what you can build yourself. No API, no code. Just Claude or ChatGPT."
Why it lands
Removes the barrier. This isn't some custom tool. It's the same AI everyone has access to.
2
Screen
Empty message box, ready to paste
Say
"You'll need sales call transcripts — three to five calls from your team. Export them from Otter, Fathom, Fireflies, or any recorder. Paste them here."
Why it lands
Sets expectation: they own their data. No sign-ups, no platforms — just their transcripts.
3
Screen
Paste this prompt (copy-paste ready below) into Claude
Say
"Then paste this prompt. It'll analyze your calls and give you exactly what we saw in the demo."
Why it lands
The prompt is the bridge. Same analysis, same output, no build required.
COPY AND PASTE THIS EXACT PROMPT: You are a sales call performance analyst. I'm going to give you [X] sales call transcripts from my team. Your job is to: 1. Identify the top 3 recurring objections across all calls 2. Flag the moment in each call where engagement dropped (if it did) 3. Score each rep on: Rapport, Objection Handling, Clarity, Needs Matching (1-10) 4. Identify which rep has a REPEATING pattern (same mistake across multiple calls) 5. Give me one specific coaching recommendation per rep - not general advice Format your output as: - Summary (2 sentences: what's the biggest problem in this team right now?) - Per-rep breakdown (one card per rep) - The one thing to fix first Here are the transcripts: [PASTE TRANSCRIPTS HERE]

Running the Analysis

4
Action
Click Send. Claude processes the transcripts (wait 30-60 seconds)
Say
"Hit enter. Claude's analyzing your team now."
Why it lands
Pacing. Keep energy up while Claude thinks.
5
Screen
Claude returns output. Scroll through to show:
— Summary (what's the biggest problem?)
— Rep-by-rep breakdown (Rapport, Objection Handling, etc.)
— Which rep has the repeating pattern
— One specific coaching note per rep
Say
"And there's your team audit. Top three objections. Who has the pattern. Specific coaching for each rep. Not a generic report. Actionable."
Why it lands
This mirrors the built demo. Same insight, no custom analytics platform required. Empowers viewers: they can do this today.

The Payoff

6
Screen
Show the coaching recommendations section of Claude's output
Say
"You get names, patterns, and one thing to fix for each rep. No listening. No 10-hour coaching sessions for general training. One focused skill gap, one focused fix."
Why it lands
Callback to the built demo's coaching note. Same promise. Available right now.
