Section 4
Call Analysis Framework

SPIN Scoring (score each 1–10 with evidence)

Situation Questions

Did the rep uncover current state before pitching?

  • 8–10: Asked 3+ situation questions before presenting anything
  • 5–7: Asked some questions but moved to pitch too soon
  • 1–4: Jumped straight to features and benefits

Problem Questions

Did they surface the real pain or accept surface symptoms?

  • 8–10: Dug beneath the stated problem to find the real business impact
  • 5–7: Acknowledged the problem but didn't probe deeper
  • 1–4: Took the prospect's first answer and moved on

Implication Questions

Did they amplify the cost of inaction?

  • 8–10: Made the prospect articulate what this problem is costing them
  • 5–7: Mentioned consequences but didn't make the prospect feel them
  • 1–4: Never established why solving this matters now

Need-Payoff Questions

Did the prospect sell themselves?

  • 8–10: Prospect said out loud why this solution matters to them
  • 5–7: Rep told the prospect the value instead of drawing it out
  • 1–4: Rep closed on features, not on prospect-stated needs

Challenger Dimensions (score each 1–10 with evidence)

Teach

Did the rep introduce a perspective the prospect hadn't considered?

  • 8–10: Reframed how the prospect thinks about the problem
  • 5–7: Shared some insight but it wasn't surprising or specific
  • 1–4: Nodded along with what the prospect already believed

Tailor

Did they connect insights to this specific prospect's situation?

  • 8–10: Every insight referenced something specific about this business
  • 5–7: Generic framing with a few personalized details
  • 1–4: Same pitch they give everyone

Take Control

Did the rep lead the conversation, or follow?

  • 8–10: Drove agenda, redirected tangents, asked for commitment
  • 5–7: Held direction most of the time but ceded ground on price/timeline
  • 1–4: Prospect controlled the call

Section 5
Key Moments to Flag
  • 📊 Talk ratio: if rep spoke more than 60% of the call, flag it
  • ⚡ Engagement shift: the exact moment energy changed (positive or negative) — quote the line
  • 🛑 First objection: how it arrived, how it was handled, whether it was resolved or bypassed
  • 💰 Price / budget objection: specifically watch for this — it kills the most deals. Note exactly when and how it came up.
  • 🤝 Closing attempt: was there a specific ask for next steps? Were they concrete and owned?

Industry-Specific Patterns (Online Coaching / Education)

  • "I've tried programs before and they didn't work" — this is a trust objection, not a price objection. Probe for what specifically failed.
  • Watch for: reps pitching the program before establishing belief that the prospect can succeed.
  • "Price" in coaching usually means "I don't believe the ROI" — push on what the cost of NOT solving this is, not on payment plans.
