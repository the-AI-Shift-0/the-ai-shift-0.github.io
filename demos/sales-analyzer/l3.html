Call Assessment
This call was lost because the prospect raised budget concerns three times, and each time the rep redirected to product features instead of addressing price directly. The rep never established authority in the opening - the prospect was conversationally engaged but never viewed the rep as a credible advisor. Combined with a weak opening frame, by the time price came up, the prospect had no reason to trust the value.
At 17:31, the prospect said: "That seems like a lot for our current situation." The rep's response added a third feature. The correct move: acknowledge the concern, then tie ROI to the specific outcome the prospect had just named. Instead, the prospect's tone shifted and never recovered.

Coaching Note
This is not a general training issue. It is one specific skill gap: pricing confidence.

Recommended focus: run 3 objection-handling role plays specifically on the "investment" moment. When the prospect raises budget, the rep must acknowledge it and anchor ROI before moving forward. No feature pivots until the concern is addressed.
