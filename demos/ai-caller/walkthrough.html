Filming guide for the demo video. Open on a second monitor while recording.

The Live Demo

1
Intro & Hook
Say (opening line)
"This AI made 23 calls today. I didn't make a single one. Let me show you what it found."
Why it lands
SMB owner is tired of doing everything themselves. This says: the tech handles the volume, so you can focus on closing.

Load Dashboard (index.html)
Screen
the-ai-shift-0.github.io/demos/ai-caller/index.html

What Sai sees
Clean dashboard with 3 stats cards: Show-Up Rate 82% | Calls Placed 23 | Confirmed Bookings 19. CRM and Twilio connected (green dots). Last updated May 12 · 3:47 PM.
~3-5 seconds total for page to fully load
3
Highlight Hero Stats
Say
"23 calls placed. 19 bookings confirmed. That's an 82% show-up rate. Let me put that in context."
Action
Pause here. Let the numbers sink in. You're establishing baseline credibility with numbers.
82% show-up rate is 2-3x the typical no-show rate for most SMBs (30-50% is the industry baseline).

Show Charts: Trend & Channel Performance

"Facebook is crushing it at 88%. Instagram 82%. Organic is 71%. I now know exactly where to put my ad budget."
Why it lands: SMB owners spend money on guesses. You're showing them data that tells them exactly where to spend next.

Click Into First Call (3:47 PM)
Action
Scroll down to the calls table. Click on the first row — 3:47 PM, Facebook Ad, "Confirmed booking." (Lead name is blurred on screen — just reference the time and status.)

WOW MOMENT COMING NEXT: The call was placed 47 seconds after the lead filled out the form. She says "I only just filled in the form."
~2 seconds to click. Page (l2.html) will load.
6
Transcript Page (l2.html) - The Wow Moment
What shows on screen
l2.html

Full conversation transcript, timestamps, and 3 insight cards (Pain Point | Sentiment | Booking).

Say
"This call was placed 47 seconds after she filled in the form. Total duration: 54 seconds. Less than a minute — form to confirmed booking. Listen to what she says right at the top."
THE WOW MOMENT:
AI: "I wanted to reach out and make sure you had everything you need."
LEAD: "Oh wow, yeah that's me! I only just filled in the form."

She was STILL ON THE PAGE when the AI called. That's speed and relevance.
Say (reading the transcript)
"The AI asked what her main challenge is. She said: 'I'm trying to scale my business but I'm stuck doing everything myself. I need systems.' AI captured that. The rep will walk into their call knowing exactly what she wants to solve. Calendar invite already sent."
The AI captured pain point (scaling bottleneck), confirmed the booking, AND briefed the rep - all in 54 seconds. Zero manual work.
If someone in comments says "isn't calling 47 seconds later creepy?":
"She loved it — she said 'I really appreciate the quick follow-up.' When someone fills out a form, they're in the moment. They want to talk NOW. Wait 2 hours and they've moved on. Speed is the point."

Focus on Rep Briefing
What's visible on l3.html

"Your 2PM Thursday Call - Lead wants to scale but is the bottleneck in their own business. Open with: 'Maya mentioned you're doing everything yourself — let's talk about how that changes.'"

Use case fit: Strong
Likely objection: Time to implement
Suggested close: Done-for-you vs DIY angle

Note: "Maya" is the AI persona name — the name the AI caller uses when it calls leads. Viewers need to know this or they'll wonder who Maya is.

"Quick note — the AI calls as 'Maya.' We gave it a name so it sounds like a real person. The rep briefing tells your closer: Maya already spoke to her, here's what she said. The closer walks in knowing her pain point, what to say, the objection to expect, the close to use. No prep. She's never touched this lead before but she has everything she needs."
Why it lands: SMB owners' teams spend 30-45 minutes prepping per call. This is instant, automated prep. That's a full day back per rep per week.

2

The AI Caller Master Trove

1
Introduce the Master Trove
Screen
Navigate to the-ai-shift-0.github.io/demos/ai-caller/master-trove
Say
"Here's what you're taking away from today. The AI Caller Master Trove."
Why it lands
Names the thing. They're about to see something concrete — not a list of tips.
2
Show the Form Briefly
Screen
Scroll through the form — industry, call volume, what triggers the AI, what the call is trying to achieve. Don't fill it out on camera.
Say
"You fill in your details here — your industry, how many calls a day, what triggers the AI to call, what you want it to achieve. Takes 2 minutes. And this is what comes out."
Why it lands
Show the form exists so they understand it's built for them. Move to the output immediately — the output is the payoff.
3
Navigate to the Example Output Page
Prep (before filming)
Open the-ai-shift-0.github.io/demos/ai-caller/example-output in a browser tab before you start filming. This page shows the full pre-generated output (cost estimate, stack, scripts) — no form needed.
Screen
Switch to the example output page (the-ai-shift-0.github.io/demos/ai-caller/example-output).
Say
"Here's what comes out."
Why it lands
Clean reveal. Don't make them watch a form being filled. Pause here — let them read the screen for 2 seconds before you start talking.
4
Walk Through Part 1 — Cost Estimate
Screen
Scroll through the cost estimate section — show the monthly breakdown by component, the one-time setup row, the vs-setter comparison
Say
"First — exactly what this costs to build. Not what someone will quote you. Calculated to your call volume: your AI voice platform, your Twilio, your calendar, your automation stack. Total monthly. One-time setup. And what that looks like compared to paying a human setter."
Why it lands
The audience has been wondering "how much does this cost?" the whole video. You're answering it specifically, on screen, calculated for their business. That's insider knowledge.
5
Walk Through Part 2 — Stack Recommendation
Screen
Scroll through the stack section — show the vendor recommendations with reasons, the "avoid" notes
Say
"Second — the exact stack. Not a list of options. A specific recommendation for your call volume, with a reason for each vendor. Including which ones to avoid. I've built this. I know which ones are traps."
Why it lands
The "which ones to avoid" line is the authority signal. Anyone can list options. Only someone who's built it knows the traps.
6
Walk Through Part 3 — Scripts (The Close)
Screen
Scroll through the scripts section — show the opening line, the objection handling, the close. Pause on the opening script line.
Say
"And this — this is the one people spend months trying to figure out. Your actual scripts. The opening line. How to handle the three most common objections. The close. All of it. Ready to paste directly into Retell AI or VAPI. Your AI is live the same day."
Why it lands
The scripts are the most concrete deliverable. Audience can visualise loading them into a platform and going live. Don't say the CTA yet — the Claude demo comes next.
7
Show It in Claude — Customize Live
Prep (before filming)
Have claude.ai open in a tab. Have the opening script from the example output copied to your clipboard.
Screen
Open Claude.ai. Paste the opening script into a new conversation. Type a customization request — e.g. "rewrite this for a prospect who already attended a webinar but didn't buy." Show Claude's response generating live.
Say
"And if you want to adapt any of it — paste the script into Claude, tell it the scenario. 30 seconds. You've got a new version built for your exact situation."
Optional add (choose one)
Option A (Authoritative): "You're going to see a lot of people try to sell you AI caller solutions. Most of them are wrappers around an API. This is a system. Stack, scripts, the whole thing."

Option B (Cheeky): "And yeah, someone's going to try to charge you $50K to build this. Maybe don't."

A is more authoritative. B is more Sai. Use whichever fits your energy on the day.
CTA
"The link is in the description. Put in your email — takes 2 minutes — and this lands in your inbox. You walk in knowing more than the person you're paying."
Why it lands
Ending on the Claude customization makes it undeniable — the audience just watched the scripts get built AND adapted live. CTA is the natural next step.
