2

The Spoken Segment (No Screen Demo)

1
The Setup - Acknowledge the Temptation
Say
"You've just seen this working. You want it. But you know what's about to happen — you're going to hire someone to build it, and they're going to charge you $40-60K for a custom app that should cost $8-12K, and then you'll find out they used the wrong tools and the whole thing falls apart in six months."
Why it lands
SMB owner has been burned before on dev projects. Sai is naming the pain: they got ripped off. He's positioning as the insider who knows better.
2
Position Sai as the Insider
Say
"I've built this. I know exactly what stack to use, what vendors to trust, what to demand from a builder, and what corners NOT to cut. I've seen the $40K failures. I know what the difference is between $40K garbage and $10K gold."
Why it lands
Authority move. You've been in the trenches. You know the difference between good and bad. You're the expert, not the upseller.
3
Transition to Offerings
Say
"So I've packaged everything you need into two things. And honestly, you only need one of them."
Tone
Conversational. Not salesy. You're giving them tools, not selling them a product.
Lead Magnet #1: The AI Caller Blueprint
What It Is
Full stack breakdown: which tools to use (AI model, voice provider, CRM connector), which vendors are solid vs. which are garbage, cost breakdown (what consultants charge vs. what it actually costs), what to demand from a builder.
Sai's Pitch
"The Blueprint is the technical roadmap. It's literally what I used. The tools I chose, the vendors I vetted, the budget line items. You get to know: this vendor is a trap, use this one instead. This feature looks cool but you don't need it. This is where 80% of projects blow budget."
Who It's For
Builders. Engineers. Tech co-founders who want to build it themselves or brief a dev team from a position of authority.
Lead Magnet #2: The AI Caller Project Brief
What It Is
A production-ready project brief you can paste into Claude or hand to a developer. Covers your use case, the architecture, API contracts, success metrics, the scripts the AI should use, CRM integration specs, everything.
Sai's Pitch
"The Project Brief is the playbook. Copy and paste it into Claude or hand it to a dev. They'll build from that brief. No guessing. No 'well what did you want exactly?' — it's all there. Use case, stack, success metrics, timelines. Either build it yourself or pay a dev with a clear spec. Either way, you're not getting ripped off."
Who It's For
Builders and SMB owners who want to hire a dev. You have a clear spec, so you can evaluate whether the price is fair. You're not guessing.
4
The Closer
Say
"Download one or both. If you're building it yourself, get the Blueprint. If you're hiring someone, get the Project Brief. Either way, you're walking into that conversation knowing more than the guy you're paying. That's power."
Short. Direct. No hard sell. You're giving them a tool that makes them smarter about their next move.
5
