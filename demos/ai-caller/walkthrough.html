AI Caller Demo Walkthrough

Filming guide for the demo video. Open on a second monitor while recording.

The Live Demo

1
Intro & Hook
Say (opening line)
"This AI made 23 calls today. I didn't make a single one. Let me show you what it found."
Why it lands
SMB owner is tired of doing everything themselves. This says: the tech handles the volume, so you can focus on closing.
2
Load Dashboard (index.html)
Screen
the-AI-Shift-95.github.io/demos/ai-caller/

Watch loading overlay fade. Page will show header, then hero stats, then charts, then calls table.

What Sai sees
Clean dashboard with 3 stats cards: Show-Up Rate 82% | Calls Placed 23 | Confirmed Bookings 19. CRM and Twilio connected (green dots). Last updated May 12 · 3:47 PM.
~3-5 seconds total for page to fully load
3
Highlight Hero Stats
Say
"23 calls placed. 19 bookings confirmed. That's an 82% show-up rate. Let me put that in context."
Action
Pause here. Let the numbers sink in. You're establishing baseline credibility with numbers.
82% show-up rate is 2-3x the typical no-show rate for most SMBs (30-50% is the industry baseline).
4
Show Charts: Trend & Channel Performance
Say
"This shows show-up rate trending up through the week. And here's where the real decision-making power comes in."
Then point to Source Performance chart
Facebook Ad: 88% | Instagram Ad: 82% | Webinar signup: 79% | Organic: 71%
Say
"Facebook is crushing it at 88%. Instagram 82%. Organic is 71%. I now know exactly where to put my ad budget."
Why it lands: SMB owners spend money on guesses. You're showing them data that tells them exactly where to spend next.
5
Click Into First Call (3:47 PM)
Action
Scroll down to the calls table. Click on the first row — 3:47 PM, Facebook Ad, "Confirmed booking." (Lead name is blurred on screen — just reference the time and status.)
Say
"Let me click into this confirmed booking to show you what actually happened."
WOW MOMENT COMING NEXT: The call was placed 47 seconds after the lead filled out the form. She says "I only just filled in the form."
~2 seconds to click. Page (l2.html) will load.
6
Transcript Page (l2.html) - The Wow Moment
What shows on screen
l2.html

Full conversation transcript, timestamps, and 3 insight cards (Pain Point | Sentiment | Booking).

Say
"This call was placed 47 seconds after she filled in the form. Total duration: 54 seconds. Less than a minute — form to confirmed booking. Listen to what she says right at the top."
THE WOW MOMENT:
AI: "I wanted to reach out and make sure you had everything you need."
LEAD: "Oh wow, yeah that's me! I only just filled in the form."

She was STILL ON THE PAGE when the AI called. That's speed and relevance.
Say (reading the transcript)
"The AI asked what her main challenge is. She said: 'I'm trying to scale my business but I'm stuck doing everything myself. I need systems.' AI captured that. The rep will walk into their call knowing exactly what she wants to solve. Calendar invite already sent."
The AI captured pain point (scaling bottleneck), confirmed the booking, AND briefed the rep - all in 54 seconds. Zero manual work.
If someone in comments says "isn't calling 47 seconds later creepy?":
"She loved it — she said 'I really appreciate the quick follow-up.' When someone fills out a form, they're in the moment. They want to talk NOW. Wait 2 hours and they've moved on. Speed is the point."
7
Highlight Insight Cards
Point to three cards below transcript
Pain Point: "Scaling bottleneck - doing everything herself, needs systems"
Sentiment: "Positive - impressed by fast follow-up and ready to book"
Booking: "Confirmed: Thursday 2PM - calendar invite sent"
Say
"Three things happened instantly: pain point captured, sentiment scored, booking confirmed. The AI didn't just make a call. It gathered intelligence."

Click "View post-call summary →" (l3.html)

l3.html will load with Lead Profile, Sentiment Score, Rep Briefing, and CRM Update.
9
Focus on Rep Briefing
What's visible on l3.html
Left column has a "Rep Briefing" box. It reads:

"Your 2PM Thursday Call - Lead wants to scale but is the bottleneck in their own business. Open with: 'Maya mentioned you're doing everything yourself — let's talk about how that changes.'"

Use case fit: Strong
Likely objection: Time to implement
Suggested close: Done-for-you vs DIY angle

Note: "Maya" is the AI persona name — the name the AI caller uses when it calls leads. Viewers need to know this or they'll wonder who Maya is.
Say
"Quick note — the AI calls as 'Maya.' We gave it a name so it sounds like a real person. The rep briefing tells your closer: Maya already spoke to her, here's what she said. The closer walks in knowing her pain point, what to say, the objection to expect, the close to use. No prep. She's never touched this lead before but she has everything she needs."
Why it lands: SMB owners' teams spend 30-45 minutes prepping per call. This is instant, automated prep. That's a full day back per rep per week.

"30% show-up before. 82% after. That's not a percentage. That's a million-five in recovered revenue. And here's the thing — clients don't even know it was AI. It sounded human because the AI isn't following a robotic script. It's reading the script I trained it on, in real time, adapting to what they say. That's the magic. It's conversational, not mechanical."
Key talking point for Sai: "The AI sounds human because it IS having a real conversation - it's just reading the script I trained it on, in real time, adapting to what the person says." This defangs the "people hate AI calls" objection.

The AI Caller Master Trove — Show It Live

1
Introduce the Master Trove
Screen
Navigate to the AI Caller Master Trove — Part 1 (the-ai-shift-0.github.io/demos/ai-caller/lead-magnet-cost)
Say
"Now — here's what I've put together for you. The AI Caller Master Trove. Two parts. Let me show you both."
Why it lands
Names the thing. The audience is about to see something, not just hear about it.
2
Show Part 1 — The Stack Breakdown
Screen
Scroll through the cost breakdown page — show the monthly cost table, the one-time developer setup row, the vendor recommendations
Say
"Part one is the stack breakdown. What this actually costs to build — not what someone will quote you. Which vendors to use, which ones are traps, where projects blow budget. I've built this. These are the real numbers."
Why it lands
Positions Sai as the insider. The audience has been wondering "how much would this actually cost?" — this answers it on screen.
3
Navigate to Part 2 — The Implementation Brief
Screen
Navigate to the-ai-shift-0.github.io/demos/ai-caller/lead-magnet-brief
Say
"Part two is the implementation brief. Fill out this form — tell it about your business, what you want the AI to do, how many calls a day. It generates a document you either paste into Claude or hand to a developer."
Why it lands
The form mechanic shows personalization. It's not a generic PDF — it's built for their specific use case.
4
Fill Out the Form Live
Action
Fill out the form on camera — use a realistic example (online coaching, $1-3M, 10-30 calls/day, trigger: someone fills out a form, objective: confirm attendance)
Say
"I'll fill this out for a coaching business — 10-30 calls a day, someone fills out a form, AI confirms attendance. Hit generate."
Why it lands
Live fill-out is more credible than a pre-filled result. The audience watches it work.
5
Show the Output — The Close
Screen
Scroll through the generated brief — show the system requirements, the recommended stack, the key questions for developers
Say
"There's your implementation brief. Paste this into Claude or hand it to a developer — they build from this. No guessing. No 'well what did you want?' — it's all there. Either you build it yourself or you hire someone, and either way you walk in knowing more than the person you're paying. That's power."
Optional add (choose one)
Option A (Consultative): "You're going to see a lot of people try to sell you AI solutions. Most of them are wrappers around an API. This is a system. It works with your CRM, your team, your offer."

Option B (Cheeky): "And yeah, someone's going to try to do this for $50K. Maybe don't."

A is more authoritative. B is more Sai. Use whichever fits your energy on the day.
Why it lands
They've just watched a live demo of the Master Trove. The CTA feels like the natural next step — not a pitch.
