The Project

What This Is

A new YouTube channel: The AI Shift. Sai on camera, showing SMB owners ($2–10M revenue) how to use AI tools in their business. Not theory — actual tool demos, screen recordings, real workflows.

Launch: June 2026  |  Cadence: 2 videos/week  |  Format: Long-form, 10–20 min

First month goal: 2 published + 8 fully edited and buffered.

Brand Voice — Important

This channel has a specific tone: direct, smart, no fluff. Not corporate, not hype. Think smart friend who works in tech and saves you from getting ripped off. The editing should match — fast, clean, confident. Nothing should feel bloated or overdone.

"Here's the tool everyone's talking about. Here's what it actually does. Here's what you'd pay. Here's why that's a bad deal for most of you."

The audience is business owners, not tech enthusiasts. Don't dumb it down, but keep effects purposeful — every graphic should help a non-technical person understand, not just look cool.

Your job in each section is to serve the footage — cut silences, tighten pacing, add graphics where they help. Your Creative Scope

Jump cut rhythm and pacing within a segment Reordering sections or moving content between sections

Graphics timing, style, and visual emphasis Cutting any on-camera segment because you think it's redundant
Music selection and level Any structural change — flag it, don't make it

Editing Standards

Captions

  • Animation: Word-by-word animated reveal. Not static white text.
  • Emphasis: Key phrases highlighted in #7c3aed (brand purple).
  • Font: Clean sans-serif, large enough to read on mobile (16px minimum).
  • Accuracy: Grammatically perfect. Native-level English. Zero spelling errors.
  • Placement: Never cover Sai's face or key screen content.

Talking Head (Sai on Camera)

  • Pacing: Cut dead air ruthlessly. If there's a 0.5s pause that adds nothing, cut it. If you're unsure whether a pause was intentional (dramatic emphasis), leave it and flag it rather than removing it.
  • Cuts: Jump cuts are fine—this audience expects them.
  • Energy: Keep it up. If a sentence sounds flat, flag it for re-record rather than papering over it with effects.
  • Effects: Minimal. Sai's delivery will improve over time—don't auto-correct with too many effects.

Screen Recordings (Demo Sections)

  • PiP: Sai in bottom-right corner. Circular crop preferred.
  • Highlights: Add zoom/pan to emphasize the part of the screen being discussed.
  • Callouts: Arrows or highlight boxes when pointing to specific UI elements (brand purple #7c3aed).
  • Cursor: Visible and smooth. Remove jitter.
  • Sound Effects: Subtle click sounds if they aid clarity. Don't overdo it.

Motion Graphics

  • Concept Breakdowns: Clean diagrams with numbered steps or flow arrows.
  • Data/Stats: Large text, animated entry, brand purple accent.
  • Frameworks: Simple boxes or flow charts. No 3D. No gradients. Flat design.
  • Lower Thirds: Sai's name (episode 1 only). Clean, simple, on-brand.

B-Roll

  • Subject Matter: Business-appropriate. Office, laptop, phone, meetings.
  • Avoid: Lifestyle stock (coffee shops, generic "people typing").
  • AI-Related: Data visualisations, code on screen, chat interfaces are fine.
  • Usage: Keep it short. Don't pad with B-roll. This is a demo channel—screen recordings are primary.

Music

  • Level: Background only. Never louder than 15–20% of voice track.
  • Type: Instrumental. No lyrics.
  • Vibe: Slightly upbeat but not gimmicky. Think "focused work" playlist, not TikTok trending audio.
  • CTA Section: Fade out before CTA so Sai's voice is clean.

Pacing

  • Target: 140–160 words per minute effective pace after edits.
  • Hook: Should feel urgent.
  • Demos: Can breathe slightly more than hook.
  • Static Screens: Never let a cut land more than 4 seconds on a static screen without narration or visual movement.

Thumbnails

Design Requirements

  • Sai's Face: Expressive, clearly visible.
  • Text Overlay: 2–5 words max. Bold, high contrast.
  • Background: Contrasting to Sai's clothing. Consider brand purple accent.
  • A/B Testing: Deliver 2 variants per video. Test performance on YouTube.

Before You Start: 5 Questions to Answer

1. Is the audio clean or does it need noise reduction?

Check: background hum, AC noise, room echo, mic quality. Flag any need for processing before editing.

2. Are the screen and webcam recordings synced?

Confirm: which file is master, are there any sync issues, drift, or frame rate mismatches?

3. Did the host flag any retakes or unusable sections?

Ask: are there parts Sai wants to skip, re-record, or fix in the rough cut?

4. What's the exact topic and main takeaway?

Confirm: title, hook, and which concepts need visual emphasis so your graphics land.

5. Are there branding assets or templates to use (fonts, colors, overlays)?

Check: provided graphics kit, approved fonts, and any existing motion templates.
